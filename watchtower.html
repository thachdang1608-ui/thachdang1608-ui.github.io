Overview

A strategic analytics initiative for fraud leadership.

Watchtower was one of GeoComply's major “Big Bets,” designed to help operator leadership teams understand fraud risks and the effectiveness of GeoComply's anti-fraud ecosystem in real time.

The goal was to transform fragmented fraud reporting into a centralized intelligence platform that could clearly communicate fraud trends, business risks, ecosystem effectiveness, and product value.

Business Goals

Supporting retention, executive reporting, and product positioning.

  • Strengthen enterprise customer retention conversations.
  • Support executive-level reporting and strategic decision-making.
  • Position GeoComply's broader anti-fraud ecosystem more clearly.
  • Create cross-selling opportunities for CMS, IDComply, and adjacent products.

My Role

I connected product strategy, fraud data, and executive dashboard UX.

UX Strategy Product Thinking Information Architecture Dashboard UX Data Visualization Stakeholder Collaboration

Business Context

Fraud reporting existed, but leadership visibility was fragmented.

GeoComply's anti-fraud ecosystem processes large-scale real-time fraud and compliance signals across multiple systems, products, and regions. However, reporting workflows were distributed through emails, Excel files, Google Sheets, and disconnected internal systems.

Existing tools were mostly built for analysts and investigation workflows. Executive stakeholders still lacked centralized fraud visibility, business-oriented insight, real-time trend monitoring, and executive-friendly reporting.

  • Difficult tracking and monitoring across teams.
  • Inconsistent reporting and metric interpretation.
  • Slow insight generation and dependency on analysts.
  • Limited real-time visibility for leadership.

The Problem

Leadership needed quick understanding without operational complexity.

Leaders needed a fast, intuitive way to understand fraud trends, severity, business risks, and ecosystem performance without relying on complex operational reporting or analyst interpretation.

At the business level, GeoComply also needed a stronger executive-facing platform to support enterprise relationships, demonstrate anti-fraud effectiveness, and enable enterprise-level decision-making.

Target Users

An executive-first dashboard with enough depth for fraud teams.

  • CEOs and COOs needed a fast read on risk posture and ecosystem health.
  • CTOs needed confidence in signal coverage, reliability, and technical feasibility.
  • CFOs needed business-risk visibility and impact-oriented reporting.
  • Fraud leadership needed trend context, anomaly visibility, and drill-down paths.
  • Enterprise buyers needed a clear product value story.

Personas

Key leadership and fraud stakeholders shaped the product direction.

Chief Compliance Officer (CCO)

Focused on fast understanding of compliance risks, fraud exposure, and business impact to protect revenue, reputation, and regulatory standing.

Chief Technology Officer (CTO)

Focused on gaining visibility into system performance, operational risks, and technology effectiveness to support strategic business decisions.

VP of Fraud

Focused on fast understanding of fraud risks, trend changes, and prevention effectiveness to protect revenue, reputation, and operational performance.

Research & Discovery

I mapped metrics, workflows, constraints, and leadership reporting needs.

I conducted discovery sessions with engineering managers, product managers, fraud teams, internal leadership stakeholders, and engineers to understand existing metrics, technically feasible metrics, valuable business KPIs, reporting pain points, and fraud analysis practices.

I also worked closely with developers to understand data flows, real-time limitations, cross-platform dependencies, and system architecture so the UX direction could remain ambitious but buildable.

Early Exploration

Lo-fi concepts tested hierarchy before visual polish.

One of the biggest UX challenges was simplifying dense fraud intelligence into an experience executives could understand within seconds. I explored multiple low-fidelity concepts to validate dashboard hierarchy, KPI prioritization, chart usefulness, real-time monitoring approaches, information density, and executive scanning behavior.

The goal was not visual polish. It was validating usability, business usefulness, technical feasibility, and stakeholder alignment before defining the final information architecture.

Early brainstorming

Initial project thinking and idea exploration before refining the dashboard hierarchy and information architecture.

Information Architecture

I redesigned the reporting structure into three primary layers.

Level 1

Executive Summary

Fraud trends, business risks, ecosystem health, high-level KPIs, and real-time indicators for fast executive scanning.

Level 2

Trend & Comparative Analysis

Trend movement, comparative metrics, regional analysis, anomaly detection, and fraud patterns for deeper strategic analysis.

Level 3

Operational Drill-Down

Analyst workflows, detailed fraud investigation, operational validation, and deeper reporting analysis.

Validation

Continuous feedback refined clarity and reporting usefulness.

The project involved recurring collaboration and validation with leadership stakeholders, PMs, engineers, and fraud analysts. Feedback helped refine KPI prioritization, chart readability, reporting hierarchy, real-time visibility patterns, and executive-focused storytelling.

Collaboration

Balancing business goals, technical feasibility, and executive usability.

I worked closely with PMs, engineers, fraud analysts, QA teams, and leadership stakeholders to align goals, validate reporting priorities, understand technical constraints, support implementation reviews, and refine the dashboard through feedback.

Stakeholder Feedback

Feedback loops helped shape the dashboard into a clearer executive product.

Throughout the project, feedback from PMs, leadership stakeholders, engineers, and fraud teams helped validate whether the dashboard was communicating the right level of detail. These reviews clarified what needed to be simplified, what needed more evidence, and which parts of the experience were strongest for executive-facing conversations.

Feedback

Hi-Fi Design

A modular dashboard system for real-time executive reporting.

The final UI focused on a clean, scalable enterprise reporting experience optimized for real-time monitoring, executive readability, anomaly visibility, and rapid decision-making.

  • Executive dashboard overview.
  • Fraud trend visualization.
  • Real-time monitoring widgets.
  • Comparative reporting.
  • Alert and anomaly patterns.
  • Operational drill-down experiences.

Design System

Reusable patterns supported future ecosystem growth.

The modular dashboard architecture supported multiple fraud domains, scalable KPI expansion, consistent reporting patterns, and future ecosystem growth.

  • Reusable visualization components.
  • Scalable KPI cards.
  • Responsive layouts.
  • Consistent spacing and typography.
  • Modular dashboard components.

Hi-Fi Dashboard Details

Chart modules translated fraud signals into executive-readable patterns.

In the high-fidelity phase, I focused on making complex fraud patterns easier to scan and discuss. The dashboard used focused chart modules to explain where risk was concentrated, how behavior shifted across regions, and which fraud signals required leadership attention.

Dashboard detail screen

A high-fidelity dashboard view showing how executive summary, fraud trends, and supporting modules worked together in a single reporting experience.

Geolocation of Confirmed ATOs

A map-based chart helped leadership quickly understand geographic concentration of account takeover activity and identify where risk needed deeper review.

Reported SPB Jumping Behavior

A comparative chart made proxy and target-state behavior easier to interpret, supporting fraud pattern analysis without forcing users into raw tables.

UX Decisions

Product thinking turned raw fraud data into actionable insight.

01

Prioritized trend visualization over raw tables

Leadership reacted faster to trend movement and anomalies than isolated operational metrics.

02

Used progressive disclosure

Executives received simplified summaries while operational teams retained deeper analysis paths.

03

Shifted reporting toward business storytelling

The dashboard explained business impact, fraud severity, and ecosystem value instead of only technical metrics.

04

Standardized KPI grouping

Consistent terminology helped different teams interpret fraud metrics in the same way.

MVP & Iteration

The product was planned across multiple release phases.

Due to ecosystem complexity, I helped define MVP scope, prioritize dashboard modules, validate technical feasibility, and align releases with business goals. After each release, stakeholder reviews were conducted, feedback was collected, improvements were identified, and designs were iterated based on real usage insights.

Outcome & Impact

A centralized fraud intelligence layer for executive understanding.

The final solution helped create a centralized fraud intelligence experience, clearer visibility into fraud trends, faster executive reporting workflows, improved communication of GeoComply's ecosystem value, and stronger alignment between leadership and operational teams.

The platform also supported enterprise demos, customer retention conversations, strategic upsell opportunities, and broader awareness of GeoComply's anti-fraud capabilities.

Business Impact

Watchtower became an important strategic product for GeoComply by helping support contract renewals with important enterprise clients, strengthen enterprise trust through clearer fraud visibility, create new customer opportunities in 2025, and establish a foundation for future dashboard and reporting products.

The project also became an important starting point for expanding executive-facing analytics experiences across the GeoComply ecosystem.

Potential Impact Metrics

  • Reduced dependency on manual reporting workflows.
  • Improved realtime visibility into fraud conditions.
  • Faster executive insight generation.
  • Consolidated reporting across multiple systems.
  • Improved strategic communication with enterprise clients.

Reflection

Watchtower became more than a dashboard.

This project reinforced the importance of designing enterprise analytics platforms around strategic decision-making, information prioritization, executive cognition, and business storytelling rather than simply displaying operational data.

It strengthened my experience in enterprise UX, real-time analytics, information architecture, data visualization, product strategy, cross-functional collaboration, and executive-focused reporting systems.
